Suzuki cappuccino

The Suzuki Cappuccino is a small, sporty two-seater convertible produced by Suzuki in the early 1990s (1991–1998). It is part of Japan’s kei car category, meaning it is very compact and lightweight, designed to meet strict size and engine restrictions. Key Features: Engine: 657cc turbocharged 3-cylinder engine (kei car limit), producing around 63 hp Transmission: 5-speed manual gearbox Drive: Rear-wheel drive (RWD), giving it a fun, sporty feel Body: Lightweight, 2-door roadster with a removable hardtop, soft top, or targa roof Dimensions: Very compact — about 3.3 meters long and 1.4 meters wide Performance: Small size and light weight (~725 kg) make it agile and fun to drive Style: Retro 1990s sporty design with pop-up headlights and a cute, minimalist roadster look Interior: Simple, driver-focused cockpit, seats two people comfortably, and minimalistic dashboard Fun Fact: Despite its tiny size, the Cappuccino is loved by car enthusiasts worldwide for its sporty handling and charm. It’s considered a classic Japanese kei sports car.
